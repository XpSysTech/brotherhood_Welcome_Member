Constitution & Rules

A plain-language, web-friendly summary of the Brotherhood Investment Fund Constitution. This page is for clarity and alignment — the authoritative constitution remains the full written document.

Constitution
Preamble +
We, the founding members of the Brotherhood Investment Fund (“the Brotherhood”), establish this Constitution to define our purpose, values, governance, and operating rules.

The Brotherhood exists to build disciplined men, strong character, and long-term financial independence through collective effort, accountability, and structured investment.

All members agree that discipline, transparency, and unity take precedence over personal interest.
Article I.A — Definitions +
Key terms used in the Constitution:
  • Brotherhood: A private, closed collective established under this Constitution.
  • Member: A founding individual admitted into the Brotherhood and in good standing.
  • Member in Good Standing: Meets obligations, participates, and is not under discipline.
  • Council: Governing body responsible for oversight and enforcement.
  • Quorum: More than half (50%) of eligible participants.
  • Simple Majority: More than 50% of votes cast.
  • Supermajority: At least two-thirds (⅔) of eligible votes.
  • Contribution: Financial payment made under the approved structure.
  • Management Fee: NAD 50/month included in contributions for admin only.
  • Pooled Capital: Total collected investment funds held collectively.
Interpretation Clause: In ambiguity, the Council’s interpretation prevails.
Article I — Name & Nature +
  1. The organization is the Boena Investment Fund.
  2. The Brotherhood is a private, closed collective — not public, not a charity, and not a financial service for outsiders.
  3. Membership is limited to founding members only, unless amended by the Constitution.
Article II — Purpose & Objectives +
The Brotherhood exists to:
  • Develop character, discipline, and responsibility among members
  • Pool capital in a structured and transparent manner
  • Invest collectively in productive assets and businesses
  • Build long-term financial resilience and independence
  • Contribute to societal upliftment through ethical action and charity
Article III — Core Principles +
All decisions and conduct are guided by:
  1. Discipline over comfort
  2. Structure over impulse
  3. Transparency over secrecy
  4. Long-term value over short-term gain
  5. Collective interest over individual benefit
  6. Accountability without favoritism
Article IV — Membership +
Eligibility: Membership is limited to founding members who commit during the establishment phase. No new members are accepted once operational unless amended by supermajority vote.

Obligations: Members must comply with rules, contribute financially as agreed, participate respectfully, act in good faith, and avoid conduct that damages trust or reputation.

Rights: Members may access transparent records, participate in discussions/votes (where applicable), raise concerns via proper channels, and be treated fairly.
Article IV.A — Active Participation (Non-Negotiable) +
Membership is active by nature, not passive. Financial contribution alone does not equal full membership. Every member is expected to contribute to success and stability through participation and service.

Contribution includes: attending meetings, sharing skills/knowledge, serving on committees, supporting execution, and upholding Brotherhood culture.

Non-contribution: repeated absence, extracting value without adding value, disengagement, or ignoring responsibilities.

Removal for non-contribution: may be initiated after opportunity to improve, reviewed by Council, the member is heard, and a vote is taken.
“The Brotherhood values active men, not silent passengers.”
Article V — Financial Structure +
Contributions: NAD 500–1,000/month during Year 1 only, including a mandatory NAD 50 management fee.

Fund control: no single person may control or withdraw funds alone. Multi-signatory controls apply. All usage must be documented and approved.

Profit allocation: Net profits are allocated as:
  • 20% retained (Growth Fund + Charity Fund)
  • 80% allocated according to member benefit rules
Article V.A — Exit, Withdrawal & Penalties +
The Brotherhood is built on long-term commitment. Exit penalties protect stability:
  • Exit within 12 months: 25% penalty
  • Exit months 13–24: 15% penalty
  • From Year 3 onward: no penalty (still subject to liquidity + Council procedure)
Exit penalties are structural safeguards, not punishment.
“Those who leave early bear the cost of instability they introduce.”
Article VI–IX — Governance & Decision Making +
The Brotherhood is governed by:
  • President (executive leadership, no unilateral financial authority)
  • Vice President (rotational continuity role)
  • Secretary (records, policies, minutes, institutional memory)
  • Council (oversight, enforcement, approvals, risk control)
  • Committees (recommendations only, no final authority)

Decision making: major decisions require Council approval; structural changes require supermajority; voting records must be documented; no decisions in secrecy.
Article X–XI — Removal & Conflict Resolution +
Removal grounds: abuse of power, financial misconduct, breach of trust, persistent non-performance, constitutional violations, or conduct harmful to unity/reputation.

Process: motion raised → investigation/hearing → right to respond → Council vote → decision recorded.

Conflict resolution: discussion → mediation → Council arbitration. Emotional public disruption is prohibited; rules override personalities.
Appendix C — Code of Conduct (Summary) +
Members must act with honesty, discipline, and respect, and place Brotherhood interests above personal gain.

Prohibited: misuse of authority, self-dealing, intimidation, misinformation, persistent disruption, and using the Brotherhood name for personal leverage without approval.

Conflicts of interest: must be declared; conflicted members recuse from discussions/votes. Failure to disclose is misconduct.
“Strength with humility, discipline with compassion, leadership with accountability.”
Note: This page is a simplified public-facing summary for alignment. The full Constitution and Appendices remain the authoritative governing documents of the Brotherhood Investment Fund.

Governance Roles & Responsibilities

This section defines the roles, responsibilities, authority, and limits of leadership and governance bodies within the Brotherhood Investment Fund.

Governance
Purpose: This framework exists to ensure clarity of responsibility, prevent concentration of power, protect collective interests, and promote discipline, transparency, and long-term continuity. All roles operate strictly within the Brotherhood’s governing charter and are subject to its rules.
President (Executive Lead) +

Primary Role: Strategic leadership and execution alignment.

Current Holder: Theodor Sageus Goagoseb

The President provides leadership while ensuring alignment between long-term strategy and day-to-day execution. The role exists to drive momentum without bypassing governance.

  • Sets and communicates long-term vision, values, and strategic direction.
  • Acts as the link between Council strategy and member or committee execution.
  • Drives execution discipline and follow-through.
  • Represents the Brotherhood externally when required.
  • Chairs Council meetings unless formally delegated.

Authority & Limits: The President may propose initiatives but may not unilaterally approve major decisions or control funds. The role is fully subject to Council oversight and removal.

Vice President (Deputy & Successor-in-Training) +

Primary Role: Continuity, support, and leadership development.

  • Acts as deputy to the President.
  • Assumes presidential duties when required.
  • Supports coordination between Council and Committees.
  • Helps enforce discipline, timelines, and execution.

The Vice Presidency is a rotational role among Council members, typically spanning six to twelve months, to prevent power concentration and prepare future leadership.

Secretary (Administration & Records) +

Primary Role: Institutional memory, documentation authority, and governance integrity.

Current Holder: Shakeel Hoosain

  • Records and distributes meeting minutes.
  • Maintains all official governance documents and records.
  • Tracks attendance, voting outcomes, and formal resolutions.
  • Ensures compliance with documentation and record-keeping standards.
  • Updates and issues amended Constitutions, policies, and official documents only after formal Council and Presidential approval.

The Secretary is a custodian of governance, not a policy-maker, and may be removed if neutrality, accuracy, or procedural integrity is compromised.

Council (Governing Body) +

Primary Role: Governance, oversight, and protection of the Brotherhood.

  • Approves strategy, investments, and major decisions.
  • Acts as a check on executive authority.
  • Oversees financial health, risk, and compliance.
  • Protects collective member interests.
  • Initiates leadership review, suspension, or removal when required.

The Council is the highest governing authority and operates through documented votes, by majority or supermajority as defined in the charter.

Council Members (Individual Duty) +

Each Council Member serves as a fiduciary guardian of the Brotherhood and is expected to act in the collective interest at all times.

  • Exercise independent judgment and oversight.
  • Uphold ethical, financial, and governance standards.
  • Participate actively in Council and committee work.
  • Serve as a link between leadership and members.
Committees (Specialized Work Units) +

Committees exist to provide focus and depth without overloading the Council.

  • Committees are chaired by Council members.
  • They may include non-Council members where appropriate.
  • They are advisory only and hold no final decision-making power.

Committees research, deliberate, and recommend. Final authority always rests with the Council.

Closing Statement: The Brotherhood’s governance structure exists to prevent power concentration, enforce accountability, protect long-term stability, and develop capable leaders over time. All members and officers are expected to uphold both the letter and the spirit of this framework.
